Evolved Logic Gates

This topic explores research undertaken by Dr. Adrian Thompson at the University of Sussex. Using a special microchip called a Field-Programmable Gate Array which allows its internal logic to be rewritten, Dr. Thompson gave the chip a task which was to differentiate between two audio tones using only 100 logic gates and without a system clock. After 4000 generations, using Darwin's principle of natural selection in removing any imperfect "offspring", the chip succesfully learned how to detect sound accurately. When trying to identify how the chip worked, they found it only used 37 of its 100 logic gates in and functioned in a way no one understood.
On the Origin of Circuits.
